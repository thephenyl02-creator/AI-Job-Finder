You are building a web app in Replit called “Legal Technology Careers for Lawyers (AI)”.

Goal:
Increase conversions by making “Upload Resume → AI Career Readiness Score in 90 seconds” the primary user flow, and add a viral sharing loop.

Build / implement these changes:

1) Homepage (Landing) — make Resume Upload the primary CTA
- Replace current hero headline and subheadline with:
  Headline: “Upload your resume. Get your AI Career Readiness Score in 90 seconds.”
  Subheadline: “See your best-fit legal tech paths, match scores for live roles, and the exact gaps to close.”
- Primary CTA button: “Upload Resume — Free”
  - Clicking opens file picker (PDF/DOCX)
  - After upload, immediately route to /score (or /dashboard) with loading state
- Secondary CTA: “Browse roles” (routes to /jobs)
- Add a short trust line under CTA:
  “Free to browse. No account needed.” + “Your resume is private.”

2) Minimal onboarding after upload (no account required)
- After upload, show a 3-step progress indicator:
  Step 1: Parse resume
  Step 2: Identify skills + experience signals
  Step 3: Match to legal tech roles
- Then show “Your AI Career Readiness Score” screen.

3) Score page (/score) — the “hook”
Show these sections in this order:
A) AI Career Readiness Score (0–100) as a ring/gauge
- Label: “Ready”, “Almost ready”, “Needs work” based on score thresholds you choose
B) Top 3 best-fit Career Paths (cards)
- Each card: Path name, “Fit: Strong/Good/Emerging”, and 2–3 bullet reasons
C) Top 5 matching jobs (cards)
- Each job: title, company, location/remote tag, match %, and “Why this match” 1–2 lines
D) Gaps to close (Top 5)
- Each gap: skill/experience area, and “How to fix in 2 weeks” micro-suggestion

4) Add a LinkedIn share loop (very important)
- Add a “Share on LinkedIn” button on /score
- Clicking should open a modal with:
  - A copy-to-clipboard post text (generated from score + top path)
  - A “Open LinkedIn” link/button that takes user to LinkedIn post composer URL (if possible) or just linkedin.com/feed
- Share text template (fill variables):
  “I just checked my Legal Tech Career Readiness — Score: {score}/100.
   Best-fit paths: {path1}, {path2}, {path3}.
   Biggest gap I’m fixing next: {gap1}.
   Try it: {your_site_url}”

5) Data collection (privacy-safe) for your future “market insights”
Store these fields (anonymized; no raw resume text stored):
- user_id (random uuid)
- upload_timestamp
- readiness_score
- top_paths (array)
- top_gaps (array)
- top_job_matches (job_ids + match%)
- optional: country/region (derived from job preferences, not from PII)
IMPORTANT:
- Do NOT store the full resume file permanently unless user explicitly saves it.
- If you must store it temporarily, delete after analysis.

6) Scoring & matching logic (simple first version)
Implement a basic scoring model using these signals from parsed resume text:
- Years of experience (approx)
- Presence of legal keywords (contracts, compliance, litigation, regulatory, etc.)
- Presence of product/ops keywords (product, roadmap, agile, stakeholder, process, metrics, etc.)
- Presence of AI/data keywords (LLM, NLP, data analysis, python, SQL, analytics, prompt, etc.)
Map the signals to:
- readiness_score (0–100)
- path_fit scores for 8–12 legal tech career paths (Legal Ops, Legal Product, Legal AI & Analytics, Legal Sales/Solutions, Compliance/GRC, Privacy, Implementation/CS, etc.)
- job match %: compare resume keyword set to each job’s keyword set

Use a transparent approach:
- Show user “Strengths” and “Gaps” based on missing clusters
- Keep it deterministic and explainable
